The OVL Test

The OVL test is a non-parametric two-sample statistical test based on the overlap coefficient (OVL).

You can test if two samples follow the same distribution.

The null hypothesis is F = G and the alternative hypothesis is F ≠ G, where F and G are the distribution functions.

You can choose either the OVL-1 test or the OVL-2 test. The OVL-1 test is equivalent to the Smirnov test.

Due to computation time issues, the two samples must have the same size.

The samples must not have duplicates.
